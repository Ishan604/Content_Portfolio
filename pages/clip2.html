About This Clip

The Dyatlov Pass incident refers to the mysterious death of nine experienced Soviet hikers in the Ural Mountains in February 1959. Led by Igor Dyatlov, the group established a camp on the slopes of Kholat Syakhl, but they never reached their destination, prompting a search that uncovered a baffling scene: their tent had been slashed open from the inside, and the hikers had fled into a sub-zero blizzard wearing little to no clothing.

While several members died of hypothermia, others were found with severe internal trauma such as crushed ribs and fractured skulls that showed no external signs of a struggle. For decades, the lack of a clear explanation fueled theories ranging from secret military testing to paranormal encounters, though modern scientific research now points to a tragic "slab avalanche" as the most likely cause of their sudden and desperate flight into the wilderness.
