The WOW Signal from Space

February 2025 38 seconds Mystrey

About This Clip

The "Wow! Signal" is a powerful, narrowband radio signal detected on August 15, 1977, by the Big Ear radio telescope at Ohio State University while scanning for signs of extraterrestrial life. Astronomer Jerry Ehman discovered the anomaly on a computer printout a few days later, circling the sequence "6EQUJ5" and writing "Wow!" in the margin, as the signal perfectly matched the expected profile of an interstellar transmission

The signal lasted for 72 seconds and originated from the constellation Sagittarius, appearing on the "hydrogen line" frequency that scientists long believed aliens would use to communicate. Despite decades of follow-up observations, the signal has never been detected again, remaining one of the strongest candidates for an alien message though recent scientific breakthroughs in 2024 and 2025 suggest it may have been a rare natural phenomenon caused by a hydrogen maser flare triggered by a distant magnetar.
